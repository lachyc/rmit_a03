When it comes to general skills required by the group, they all follow suit quite closely. All job titles require productive communication, onfidence and tenacity, comprehensive problem-solving skills as well as good people skills. Writing is another one; however, writing in a sense where they have to be proficient in drafting paragraphs and summaries of situations or design propositions more so than writing formal letters to individuals. However, for something such as Senior Developer or Strategic and International Policy Officer both are in fact necessary.

IT specific skills vary widely; however, the skillset would include an array of languages such as C++, Python, Java and HTML for web design and development as well as non-language skills such as proficiency in signals analysis, Unity graphics design proficiency and encryption and decryption proficiency for Senior Development and Strategic and International Policies Officers.

Employers can either look for very specific skills that are only relevant to that exact job title or they could be interested in skills that are used broadly across all fields of IT. It is hard to accurately say which is more important than the other. In saying this, the online world has become more complex and therefore more dangerous than ever; data theft, data heists and exploitation of information has never been more prevalent. This puts Proficiency in Data Encryption and Decryption at the top of the hierarchy, from actions as simple as password protecting certain files to extensive MD5 hash and brute force resistant protective procedures; it seems nothing is more important these days than protecting data. This would be ranked highest when it comes to demand from employers.

Closely following this would be language skills (coding languages) as these are relevant in almost all sectors of IT. Java, C++, Python and HTML are all used frequently for many different reasons, the fact that these languages can be used to both build something as simple as a single page website but as extensive as a communications platform for a multiplayer game goes to show that they are highly desirable and therefore in high demand from employers. These would all be ranked equal second in demand.

Both Signals Analysis and Unity graphics design proficiency are in the same boat so to speak. They are both very desirable as Signals Analysis is mandatory for the profession and Unity proficiency is the gold standard for graphics design however they are only relevant in their specific field. Outside of their field they are not the most imperative skills in a general IT environment. This is why they’re ranked equal third in demand from employers.

When it comes to the general Skills, there is less ambiguity in the ranking for demand by employers. Without a doubt communication skill is in highest demand, without these skills’ productivity would be minimal and ineffective. Communication Skills are in very high demand and a critical employment point for potential employers. Communication Skills are therefore ranked first and foremost.

Problem Solving Skills follow in the tracks of Communication Skills, Problem Solving Skills are required of not just team leaders but all employees in order to achieve team cohesion within the workplace. Problem Solving Skills comes in at a close second in demand from employers.

Finally Writing skills, writing skills are important in not just any sector of IT but any sector in business as a whole. In IT however relevant departments play their role in the team environment, an individual that has the skills to perform IT specific skills with great success and also pertains the required communication skills to communicate to relevant team members can get away with having a lower writing skill level as they can communicate their information effectively to another individual that may be much more proficient in actually composing a piece of writing but have less skill in the field being written on. Where one individual may excel in a certain field the other may in another, in this case writing fits this agenda. This is unlike Communication Skills and Problem-Solving skills where it is imperative that all parties are very proficient in both of these skills. This is why Writing Skills comes in at third in terms of demand from employers.

Business Management, Business Analysis and Technical support are the three highest ranked IT-Specific skills not required in our skill set. This is understandable seeing as none of our ideal jobs directly utilise these skills, both Business Management and Business Analysis are more corporate department roles that do not directly apply to specialist roles such as the ones we are discussing. Additionally, Technical Support may apply to website design however not to the job Frontend Web Design that focuses on building and establishing the website rather than maintaining said website. These are all understandably not directly required in our skillset.

When it comes to general skills that are not required, Troubleshooting, Research and Creativity are the three highest ranked. Again, this is understandable seeing as our Ideal Jobs require specialty skills for a specialty job, the job description is not ambiguous. Troubleshooting is for bug hunters or Alpha/Beta Testers, R&D is for someone specifically in the R&D department of these corporations and Creativity is a precursor to most of these roles; an example being that the creative design would already be done prior to a Frontend Web Designer beginning their coding or a Senior Developer would oversee the development of a creative project; however, it is not their requirement to micromanage and get involved in the creative aspects of a project as they must treat all sections of the project with an equal amount of attention.
