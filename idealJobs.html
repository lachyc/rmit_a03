The differences in the above job roles are immense and truly highlight how diverse the IT industry is. There are thousands of distinct IT roles available globally, each requiring unique skill sets that may be specially developed over many years; however, in the above sample data set some consistently required skills already leap out. Being ready and willing to learn new skills that the job requires is a common theme, which makes sense as most IT professionals specialise in a distinct area and as that area changes and develops a specialist needs to be able to learn and adapt with the cutting edge. Another common element among the above jobs is that excellent written and verbal communication is required. Gone are the days of the cliché ‘basement dwelling IT nerds’, these days IT professionals are the glue that holds the modern world together and are often part of large teams that require clear threads of communication to function smoothly and efficiently. This segues finely into the final major common thread between the job descriptions which is the ability to work in a team. IT work in the 21st century is increasingly complex and specialised; therefore, companies require a team of specialists to work together towards a goal. If an IT professional cannot function well in a team-based environment, they may hold the whole company back from achieving its goals. For this reason, it makes sense that so many IT job descriptions call for a solid ability to work in a team.
